Master's Thesis Research

Confidential

Automated Data Analysis with Large Language Models for Industrial Applications

Institution: UiT, the Arctic University of Norway
Department: Computer Science and Computational Engineering
Completion: May 2025
Grade: B

Research Focus

Developed novel automated data analysis frameworks integrating Large Language Models with domain-specific processing techniques for warehouse robotics applications. Conducted in collaboration with industry partner HHS Robotics.

Key Achievements
43.5% improvement in anomaly detection precision
97.1% reduction in training time
37.2% improvement in document processing accuracy
98% accuracy in factuality verification
Technical Approach
  • Context-Augmented Anomaly Detection (CAAD) with statistical modeling
  • Ensemble methods combining Isolation Forest, One-Class SVM, and Local Outlier Factor
  • Fine-tuned GPT models for maintenance recommendation generation
  • Multimodal document processing with layout-aware PDF analysis
  • Mathematical frameworks for factuality verification using TF-IDF and entity overlap
Industry Impact
  • 68-78% reduction in documentation search time during troubleshooting
  • Enhanced maintenance scheduling with actionable recommendations
  • Reduced maintenance costs through decreased false alarms
  • Improved knowledge transfer and reduced dependency on experienced personnel

Confidentiality Notice: This research contains proprietary information and trade secrets. Detailed technical specifications, algorithms, and implementation details are protected under confidentiality agreements with industry partners.
